Smishing


Smishing is along the same vein as phishing and vishing, however it is presented through text messages.
Smishing attacks are typically aiming to gain personal information from the receiver that the attacker can then use to do such things as break passwords to any online accounts that the victim may have.
With smishing there is usually a sense of urgency from the sender, attempting to trigger a stress response from the victim making them less likely to question their intentions and comply with what is being asked.
With the rise of Bring Your Own Devices (BYOD) in companies, smishing is becoming a threat to companies as well as individuals.

Ways to prevent Smishing

Ways to mitigate a smishing attack include:

  • STOP and think, again the best piece of advice to protect yourself from social engineering attacks is to not react on instinct. Stop and think before acting.
  • Don't respond to any text messages that come through from unknown numbers without confirming who the sender is,
    this can be done by looking up the phone number in a search engine such as google, or if you have a smart device, use apps such as TrueCaller which will flag any known scam phone numbers and can add them to a blocked list so they cannot contact you.
