Phishing

o What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of Social Engineering attack that attempts to gain a victims money or identity, by manipulating individuals into revelaing personal infomation by following fraudulent links.
It is delivered by fraudulent emails, claiming to be from a trusted source but is sent with the intention of tricking the receiver into divulging confidential information such as bank details.
Social engineers masqeurade as reputable companies or people that the target is aware of, in a fake email contating a link or links to a fraudulent website.

Ways to prevent phishing

The best piece of advice for preventing social engineering attacks is to STOP, take a minute and think about the situation you're being presented with and act with caution.

Things to look out for as indicators of a phishing email include:
1. Make sure that the address that an email has been sent from matches who the email claims to be from.
To do this when reading emails on a laptop, desktop or tablet; when you open the email the senders name will be visible and next to that should be the email address that sent the email.
If it has come from a company then the company name should be in the address.



If you are viewing emails on your phone; you can click on the name of the sender and it will show you the address.

2. Don't click on any links in emails that you're not sure are legitimate.
3. Learn about the typical types of emails that are used as phishing and the signs to lookout for.
Social engineers will often try to manipulate thier targets using urgency or trying to play on their emotions.
Emails that have a sense of urgency about them, offers that seem to good to be true with shoft time limits on them for example are often used in phishing.
If you receive an email claiming to be from someone you know needing urgent financial help, try to contact that person via a method that you know is definately them and confirm if the request is genuine.
If you come across an email telling you to "act now" or creates a sense of urgency, take a moment to think, "is this likely to be genuine" before you act.
4. Be aware of emails that are received from new contacts, they won't always be phishers but again it's always best to take the time to think before you act.
5. Poor spelling, or badly written emails can also be an indication of a phishing email.
6. If your email provider has placed a message on the email stating that the "sender could not be verified" then it's best not to trust the email.
There may have been something that has flagged in the email header, the data sent along with the email.
